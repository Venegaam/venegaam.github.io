These are the wines we currently have somewhere in the process between sitting in a box as juice, concentrate, or whatever, and being drank by you. If a wine is on this page it is either available for purchase, or in some cases expected to be delivered to club members only in a future release. Some were made before I kept studious records of all my wine, and some before I could even get a good reading on acidity, so not all wines have complete information.


Thoughout the process I will endeavor to keep this page updated with the progress and my thoughts about the wines so far. We always hold back a few bottles to drink ourselves over time so that we can see how they are aging, so be sure to check back from time to time, even if you have already read about one of the wines you already have. We may be able to update our notes to even include a recommendation on when to drink it.


Have your own thoughts about the wine? Please feel free to let us know what you think by contacting us. You never know, your thoughts or notes could even make it onto this webpage.

A remake of my very first batch of wine, we thought it only fitting that this wine should be included in Nouille Cellars' first club release as well. Made using a blackberry juice base, with some blackberry puree thrown in for good measure, this wine is bursting with hints of... well... blackberries! Don't let the fact that it is a fruit wine fool you though, we have added enough sugar for the alcohol content of this wine to stand up to your everday table wine. Smooth and easily drinkable, this isn't our first time making this wine and experience says that it only gets better with age; so open it now, save it for later, we hope you enjoy this one as much as we do. Makes for a great sipper by itself, or paired well with foods you would typically pair a pinot noir with.


Unfortunately the only bottles left of this one are for our upcoming club release and a limited number of tastings.

Just in time for the summer, Nouille Cellars is happy to bring you the wine you didn’t realize was missing for that hot day on the patio. With flavors of mango and tangerine sandwiched between peach, apricot, and a little bit of apple, this wine’s surprisingly low acidity is a smooth and easy drinker. Best served with a slight chill, about 20 minutes in the fridge will do, the wine is best enjoyed as a sipping wine or paired with lighter cheeses and appetizers.
